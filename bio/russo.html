RJ has over 20 years of law enforcement experience with the City of Portsmouth New Hampshire Police Department. RJ has been trained in interview and interrogation techniques as well as investigations. RJ has investigated crimes that included homicide investigations, child abuse and neglect, sexual assaults and narcotics. As a Detective Sergeant, he was the lead supervisor of an undercover drug unit. This included management and assistance with surveillance, undercover operations, tracking and apprehension of drug dealers. RJ was also in charge of the dignitary visits to the city. This included dignitaries such as former Secretary of State Hilary Clinton, Former President Bill Clinton, and Senator John McCain. In 2009 RJ was appointed liaison to the Secret Service for a visit by President Barack Obama.

RJ rose through the ranks and retired as a Lieutenant in the Patrol Division. He was responsible for the daily operation of a twelve-person patrol shift including the review and approval of every police report that was taken during the shift.

RJ’s Executive Risk Management and security experience also stretches into the private sector. RJ is part of a four-person risk management and assessment team that works with the University of Oregon Football Team. RJ worked with the team at the 2011 BCS National Championship game, 2012 Rose Bowl and the 2013 Fiesta bowl. Also, For the past four years RJ has provided Executive Security with the Manning Passing Academy. The high school age football camp houses twelve hundred campers from around the world. There are twenty-five Divisions 1 college quarterbacks from around the country and The First Family of the NFL, The Manning’s, Archie, Peyton, Eli and Cooper.
